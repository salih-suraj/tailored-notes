## 1. Signing In

1. Open TailorEd Notes on your device.
2. Enter your **email address** and **password**, then tap **Sign in**.
3. **Managers and Inspectors only** — after signing in you will be taken to a Two-Factor Authentication screen:
   - **First time:** The app shows a secret key. Open Google Authenticator (or any TOTP app), tap Add Account, choose "Enter a setup key", and type in the key shown. Then enter the 6-digit code the app generates and tap **Verify**.
   - **Every time after that:** Just enter the current 6-digit code from your authenticator app and tap **Verify**.
4. You are now in the app.

> **Forgotten your password?** Contact your manager. Staff accounts are created by the home manager — self-registration is not available.

## 3. Children List

The **Children** tab shows every child in your home.

- Each card shows the child's name, age, room, and a coloured dot indicating their documentation status for the current shift:
  - **Green** — all required sections completed
  - **Amber** — some sections still to complete
  - **Red** — significant sections outstanding
- If any children have incomplete documentation, an amber banner appears at the top listing their names.
- **Tap any child** to open their profile.
- **Managers only:** tap the **+** icon in the top right to add a new child.

---

## 4. Child Profile

The child profile is your hub for everything related to that child. It shows:

- **Photo, name, age, and room**
- **Shift progress bar** — how much of this shift's documentation is complete
- **Alert card** — if anything is outstanding or critical (e.g. missing observation, no bath temp recorded)
- **Care record modules** — tap any module to open it

### Available modules

| Module | What it's for |
|--------|--------------|
| Daily Notes | Shift observations and welfare updates |
| Bath Temperature | Pre-bath water temperature check |
| Medication | MAR chart — scheduled meds and PRN |
| Medical History | Diagnoses, allergies, healthcare contacts |
| Behaviour | ABC behaviour incident logging |
| Sleep Diary | Bed time, settled time, wake time, quality |
| Food Diary | Meals, appetite, dietary concerns |
| Activities & Achievements | Activities, reward chart points, highlights |
| Smart Steps | Developmental goals and progress notes |
| Care Plans | Child-centred plans and goals |
| Incidents | Formal incident reports (replaces Datix) |

## 5. Daily Observations

Reached via **Daily Notes tab → tap a child** or **Child Profile → Daily Notes**.

### Viewing observations
- All observations for that child are listed, most recent first.
- Use the **shift filter chips** at the top (Morning / Afternoon / Night / Waking Night) to view only a specific shift.

### Adding an observation
1. Tap the **+ Add observation** button (bottom right).
2. Select the **shift** this observation relates to.
3. Write your observation in the text box.
4. **Voice input** — tap the microphone icon to dictate. Tap again to stop. The text is added automatically.
5. Tap **Save**.

### Editing or deleting
- Tap the **edit icon** on an observation card to edit it.
- Tap the **delete icon** and confirm to remove it permanently.

## 6. Bath Temperature

Reached via **Child Profile → Bath Temperature**.

Records water temperature before bathing to meet safeguarding requirements.

### Recording a temperature
1. Tap **+ Record temperature**.
2. Enter the temperature in °C.
3. Add any notes if needed.
4. Tap **Save**.

The record is colour-coded automatically:
- **Blue** — below 37°C (too cold — check before bathing)
- **Green** — 37–40°C (safe range)
- **Red** — above 40°C (too hot — risk of scalding, do not bathe)

One temperature record per day counts as the bath temp section being complete.

## 7. Medication (MAR)

Reached via **Child Profile → Medication**.

### Viewing the MAR
The screen is split into two sections:
- **Scheduled Medications** — medicines given at set times (e.g. morning, evening)
- **PRN (As Required)** — medicines given when needed

Each prescribed medication shows the name, dose, frequency, and how many times it has been administered today.

### Recording an administration
1. Tap **Record** on a medication.
2. Select the **outcome**:
   - **Administered** — medication was given successfully
   - **Refused** — child refused (enter a reason)
   - **Held** — dose was not given for a clinical reason (enter a reason)
3. Add any notes.
4. Tap **Save**.

### Adding a prescribed medication (managers/team leaders)
Tap the **+** icon in the top right, fill in the medication name, dose, route, frequency, and any special instructions.

> The shift completion bar only counts as done when **all** scheduled medications for this shift have been recorded.

## 8. Behaviour Incidents

Reached via **Child Profile → Behaviour**.

Uses the ABC model (Antecedent → Behaviour → Consequence) required by Ofsted.

### Logging an incident
1. Tap **+ Log incident**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Antecedent** — what happened before? (environment, triggers, activity)
   - **Behaviour** — describe exactly what the child did
   - **Consequence** — how did staff respond? What happened after?
3. Select **Severity** (Mild / Moderate / Severe)
4. Enter location, duration, and date/time.
5. Toggle safety flags if relevant:
   - **Physical intervention used** — turns red when enabled; requires detail
   - **Injury occurred** — turns red when enabled; requires detail
6. Tap **Save**.

> Physical intervention and injury flags are highlighted in red on the record card and appear as alerts in the handover summary and manager dashboard.

## 9. Sleep Diary

Reached via **Child Profile → Sleep Diary**.

### Logging a sleep record
1. Tap **+ Log sleep**.
2. Set:
   - **Bed time** — when the child went to bed
   - **Settled / fell asleep** — when they actually fell asleep
   - **Wake time** — when they woke up
3. Enter the **number of wakings** during the night.
4. Select **Sleep quality** (Poor / Fair / Good).
5. Add any notes.
6. Tap **Save**.

---

## 10. Food Diary

Reached via **Child Profile → Food Diary**.

### Logging a meal
1. Tap **+ Log meal**.
2. Select the **meal type** — Breakfast, Morning Snack, Lunch, Afternoon Snack, Dinner, Evening Snack, or Drink. The app pre-selects the most likely meal based on the time of day.
3. Describe **what was eaten**.
4. Select **Appetite** (Good / Fair / Poor).
5. Note any **concerns** (e.g. refused vegetables, only ate half).
6. Tap **Save**.

---

## 11. Activities & Achievements

Reached via **Child Profile → Activities & Achievements**.

### Logging an activity
1. Tap **+ Log activity**.
2. Select a **category** (e.g. Physical, Creative, Educational, Social, Life Skills, Therapeutic, Leisure).
3. Enter the **activity title** (e.g. "Trip to the park").
4. Add details and duration if relevant.
5. Toggle **Reward point earned** if the child earned a reward chart point for this.
6. Add an **Achievement highlight** for anything notable (e.g. "First time swimming independently") — these appear with a trophy badge on the card.
7. Tap **Save**.

## 12. Smart Steps

Reached via **Child Profile → Smart Steps**.

Smart Steps are developmental goals tracked over time. Steps are grouped into **In Progress**, **Not Started**, and **Achieved**.

### Adding a new step
1. Tap **+ Add step**.
2. Select a **category** (e.g. Communication, Self-care, Social Skills).
3. Enter the **step/goal** (e.g. "Can pour a drink independently").
4. Add a description, set a target date, and choose an initial status.
5. Tap **Save**.

### Logging progress
1. Tap a step to open its detail screen.
2. Tap **Log progress**.
3. Write a progress note describing what happened.
4. If the goal has been reached, select **Achieved** — the step will automatically move to the Achieved section.

## 13. Care Plans

Reached via **Child Profile → Care Plans**.

### Viewing a care plan
Tap any plan from the list to see the full detail including all goals and their status.

### Creating a plan
1. Tap **+ New Care Plan**.
2. Enter a **plan title** (e.g. "Q1 2026 Care Plan").
3. Set a **review date** and **status** (Active / Under Review / Completed / Archived).
4. Add **goals** using the + button at the bottom of the editor:
   - Select a goal category
   - Write the goal
   - Add a support strategy (how staff will help)
   - Set goal status and any progress notes
5. Tap **Save**.

## 14. Medical History

Reached via **Child Profile → Medical History**.

Displays the child's health information including diagnoses, allergies, and healthcare contacts.

> **Allergies appear in a red banner** at the top — always check this before administering any medication or food.

### Editing health information
Tap **Edit** (pencil icon) to update:
- NHS number, blood type
- Known allergies and adverse reactions
- Conditions and diagnoses
- Immunisation notes
- Additional medical notes

### Adding a healthcare contact
Tap **+ Add contact**, select the contact type (GP, Dentist, Psychiatrist, etc.), and fill in their name, role, phone, and address.

**Tap a phone number** to call it directly from the app.

## 15. Incident Reports

Reached via **Child Profile → Incidents**.

Formal incident reports replacing the Datix system.

### Reporting an incident
1. Tap **+ Report Incident**.
2. Select the **incident type**:
   - Behaviour Incident
   - Medical Emergency
   - Accident / Injury
   - Property Damage
   - Missing Person
   - Safeguarding
3. Select **Severity** (Low / Medium / High / Critical).
4. Enter a **title** (brief summary) and full **description** of what happened.
5. Describe the **immediate action taken** by staff.
6. Add injury details if applicable.
7. Tick the relevant **notification checkboxes**:
   - Police notified
   - Parent/guardian notified
   - Manager notified
8. If follow-up is required, toggle **Follow-up required** and describe what actions are needed.
9. Tap **Save**.

> Safeguarding and critical incidents appear as priority alerts on the Manager Dashboard.

## 16. Visitor Log

Reached via the **Checklists tab**.

The visitor log records all arrivals and departures at the home.

### Logging a visitor
1. Tap **+ Log visitor**.
2. Enter the visitor's **name** and **relation** to the child (if applicable).
3. Describe the **purpose of visit**.
4. Enter who **authorised** the visit.
5. Set the **arrival time** (defaults to now).
6. Tap **Save**.

### Recording a departure
When a visitor leaves:
1. Find their record in the list (they will show an ON SITE badge).
2. Tap **Record departure** — the current time is saved as the departure time.

## 17. Handover (Team Leaders)

Reached via the **Handover tab** (Team Leaders and Managers).

### Shift Overview
The Handover tab shows the current shift status for every child:
- **Needs Attention** — children with outstanding documentation sections listed
- **Fully Documented** — children with all sections complete

Tap any child to go directly to their profile and complete missing records.

### Handover Summary
Tap **Handover Summary** to generate an automatic written summary of the entire shift. No manual typing required — the summary pulls from all records entered during the shift, including:
- Observations per child
- Behaviour incidents (with flags for physical intervention)
- Incident reports (with severity)
- Medication outcomes
- Bath temperature

This summary can be shared with the incoming team to brief them on the shift.

## 18. Manager Dashboard

Reached via the **Dashboard tab** (Managers only).

The dashboard gives a real-time overview of the home:

- **Priority Alerts** — safeguarding flags, critical incidents, physical interventions, medication refusals — tap to see which child
- **Documentation Status** — how many children are fully documented this shift; names of those still outstanding
- **Incidents Today** — count of incidents by severity
- **Behaviour Today** — count of incidents by severity

### Behaviour Patterns
Tap **Behaviour Patterns** to open the analytics dashboard:
- **Daily trend** chart for the selected period (7/14/30 days)
- Breakdown by shift, severity, and child
- Top 5 most common antecedents (triggers)
- Filter by individual child

## 19. Settings

Reached via the **Settings tab** (all roles).

### Appearance
Choose between **System default**, **Light**, or **Dark** theme. The setting applies immediately.

### Account
Your name, email address, and role badge are shown at the top.

To sign out, tap **Sign out** and confirm. You will be returned to the login screen.

> Signing out on a shared device protects children's records. Always sign out at the end of your shift.

## Quick reference — documentation checklist per shift

For each child on your shift, aim to complete the following before handover:

- [ ] At least one **Daily Observation**
- [ ] **Bath Temperature** recorded (if child is bathed this shift)
- [ ] All **scheduled medications** recorded (given, refused, or held)
- [ ] **Behaviour incidents** logged if any occurred
- [ ] **Incident report** filed if any incident occurred
- [ ] **Sleep record** (night shift / waking night)
- [ ] **Meals** logged for your shift

The green completion dot on the child's card will show when all required sections are done.
